Amelia Earhart

Pioneering Aviator and Advocate for Women in Aviation

Here's a time line of Amelia Earhart's life:

  • 1897 - Born on July 24 in Atchison, Kansas, Earhart displayed an early interest in aviation.
  • 1921 - Began taking flying lessons and earned her pilot's license, becoming one of the few female aviators at the time. aviation.
  • 1928 - Gained international fame by becoming the first woman to fly across the Atlantic, albeit as a passenger.
  • 1932 - Accomplished the historic solo flight across the Atlantic, setting numerous records for distance and time.
  • Throughout Career - Actively promoted women's involvement in aviation and challenged gender stereotypes.
  • 1937 - Disappeared on July 2 over the Pacific Ocean during an attempt to circumnavigate the globe with navigator Fred Noonan. Their fate remains unknown, leading to enduring theories and speculation.
